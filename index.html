I used to live in a coal warehouse. It wasn't a nice life. Luckily I was rescued! Now I'm in the animal shelter and I'm looking for a quiet home where I can relax.
When I first arrived here, I was very scared. I'm still easily frightened, but I let people touch me - at least when I see that they don't mean me any harm. I don't like it on my head, though. Hello, I'm a Jacko
Jacko came to us from a shelter. Unfortunately, we have no further information about his previous home. He currently lives in a small group of African grey parrots and is doing well there.
We would like Jacko to have an experienced home that already has a female African grey parrot or a harmonious group. He is very attentive, curious and explores everything very carefully. However, he is also quick to use his beak in various situations. He probably hasn't learned any other way. His future owners should therefore be familiar with the body language of African grey parrots in order to minimize this behavior.
Since Jacko likes to be on the move and is rarely found in one place, he needs a lot of space. The aviary should therefore offer enough space - a normal standing aviary would be too small. In the animal shelter, he currently lives in an aviary with a heated interior and an outdoor aviary, which he also likes to use when the weather is good. This type of housing would also be ideal in his new home. A room of his own that offers enough space would also be an option, but must be assessed individually.

Hello, I'm a Maya
Maya is a boa constrictor that is around 1.75 m long and weighs 2.7 kg. She needs a tropical terrarium that should be at least 2 m long, 2 m wide and 2 m high. Since the animals' housing is an important factor that often leads to them being given away, we are happy to provide support and advice on choosing the right housing.
Maya is a very active snake who likes to use the entire area of ​​her terrarium and also wants to make contact with the people around her. To keep her, you need proof of expertise and a landlord's permit, and depending on the state, you may also need a permit from the local nature conservation authority.
